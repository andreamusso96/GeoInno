What explains differences in concentration in large cities ?



Andrea Musso, Dirk Helbing and Frank Neffke
Some kind of random growth process generates Zipf's law \[S_{t+1} = \gamma_t S_{t} \] \[ \ \ \ \ \ \gamma_t \sim N(\mu_t, \sigma_t) \]
Differences in Zipf exponents are consequence of differences in growth processes
Four stylized facts:
  • Downward sloping growth rates pre-urbanization
  • Upward sloping growth rates during urbanization
  • Flat sloped growth rates post-urbanization
  • Downward sloping variance
